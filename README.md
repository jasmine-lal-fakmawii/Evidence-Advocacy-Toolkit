# Evidence to Advocacy Toolkit

**By Ground Truth Institute**

A free, open-source web tool that helps local CSOs, CBOs, and NGOs in fragile and conflict-affected contexts turn their field evidence into three ready-to-use documents:

- A **project concept note** for funding and partnership
- A **donor advocacy brief** to influence how donors structure funding
- A **digital advocacy post** for websites, social media, and professional platforms

---

## Who this is for

Local programme managers in fragile, conflict-affected, and low-income contexts who:

- Have field evidence but lack the capacity or resources to turn it into professional documents
- Are currently funded as sub-implementers through INGOs or UN agencies and want to access direct donor funding
- Want to tell their own story publicly and be seen, heard, and credited for the work they do
- Are advocating for better funding structures and more equitable partnerships

Built specifically for the realities of organisations working in Myanmar and Southeast Asia, including those under low-profile or security-constrained conditions.

## What the toolkit generates

After completing 6 guided stages (15 to 20 minutes), users receive:

**Project Concept Note**
A professional funding or partnership document pre-filled with their answers, including evidence, outcomes, funding structure description, and specific ask. Ready to edit and send to donors, INGOs, or UN agencies.

**Donor Advocacy Brief**
A policy-facing document designed to influence how donors structure funding, evidence requirements, and partnerships with local organisations. Based on recommendations from field research in Myanmar and the Grand Bargain localisation agenda.

**Digital Advocacy Post**
A shorter shareable version (social media format) or longer version (website / professional platform) designed to help organisations build their public profile and tell their own story. Includes a safety check for low-profile organisations.

Each document includes:
- A safety check note relevant to the organisation's security profile
- Clear editor instructions for filling in bracketed content before sharing
- A "where to submit" guide with specific platforms, portals, and contacts

## The evidence behind this toolkit

This toolkit is grounded in:

- Primary field research with 40+ local CSOs and NGOs in Myanmar (2024 to 2025)
- Key informant interviews, focus group discussions, and surveys with local and international organisations
- The policy brief: *"Advancing Local Leadership in Myanmar's Social Protection Programmes: What Can Donors Do?"* (Fakmawii, 2025, STAAR Facility / FCDO)
- Field research across Rakhine, Kachin, Shan, Kayah, and Kayin states

Key findings that shaped the toolkit design:
- Most local organisations have limited or no formal documentation due to conflict constraints, not incapacity
- The specific description of the funding structure (how much reaches local actors, who absorbs indirect costs, who gets credit) is the most powerful evidence for localisation advocacy
- Communications and visibility gaps are a structural problem caused by unequal access to communications budgets, not by lack of local expertise
- Sector hopping is a consequence of the funding model, not a choice

## Privacy and safety

**No data is stored, transmitted, or logged.** Everything the user enters stays on their device and is cleared when they close or refresh the browser.

The toolkit includes a dedicated safety and visibility stage that:
- Flags specific security risks for organisations in conflict-affected contexts
- Distinguishes between low-profile organisations (who need to protect their identity) and publicly visible organisations (who should be empowered to tell their own story)
- Includes specific warnings about beneficiary identification, location disclosure, and political references

## Customisation

This toolkit is a single HTML file with no dependencies beyond a CDN-hosted icon library. To customise it:

- Open `index.html` in any text editor
- Update the Ground Truth Institute branding and contact details
- Add or modify questions in the `S` (stages) array in the JavaScript
- Update the "where to submit" guidance in the `whereData` object to reflect your specific context or geography
- Translate the interface by replacing English text strings

Contributions and pull requests are welcome.

---

## Adapting for other contexts

This toolkit was designed for Myanmar and Southeast Asia but the framework applies to any fragile or conflict-affected context where local organisations face:

- Multi-layered funding structures that absorb resources before they reach local actors
- Short-term project cycles that prevent institutional development
- Evidence requirements designed for stable contexts
- Communications gaps that make local work invisible in donor spaces

To adapt for a specific country or context, update the "where to submit" guidance and the contextual hints within each stage.
